Indian Rosewood (Sheesham)

Botanical Name:

Dalbergia sissoo

Common Names:

Sheesham, Indian Rosewood, Sissoo, Shisham

Description:

Indian Rosewood is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent. It is known for its strong and durable hardwood, making it ideal for furniture and construction.

Uses:

  • High-quality furniture
  • Musical instruments
  • Boat and ship building
  • Flooring and decorative veneers

Wood Properties:

  • Hard, heavy, and durable
  • Rich reddish-brown color
  • Resistant to termites and decay

Ecological Importance:

Indian Rosewood improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation and provides shade and shelter for wildlife.

Cultural Significance:

The wood is traditionally used in temples and for religious artifacts due to its strength and sacredness in Indian culture.

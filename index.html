Indian Rosewood (Sheesham)

Botanical Name:

Dalbergia sissoo – A fast-growing tree native to the Indian subcontinent, known for its robust wood and cultural value.

Common Names:

Sheesham, Indian Rosewood, Sissoo, Shisham

Description:

Indian Rosewood is a medium to large deciduous tree, typically reaching heights of 20–25 meters. The tree is admired for its graceful structure, deep-rooting system, and beautiful, strong hardwood. The leaves are compound and shed in winter. It produces fragrant white or pinkish flowers in spring and pods in summer.

Uses:

  • Used in crafting luxury furniture such as sofas, dining sets, and wardrobes due to its polish and strength.
  • Ideal for traditional Indian instruments like sitar, tabla, and harmonium.
  • Used in boat building for its water-resistance and ability to withstand humid conditions.
  • Frequently used in hardwood flooring and luxury interior paneling.

Wood Properties:

  • Hard, dense, reddish-brown wood with deep veins
  • Highly durable, termite and weather resistant
  • Finishes well with polish or varnish
  • Used in carving, turning, and cabinetry

Ecological Importance:

Sheesham trees are nitrogen-fixing and enhance soil fertility. They prevent erosion, support agroforestry systems, and provide shelter and food for birds and bees. Their shade helps in rural landscapes and contributes to ecological balance.

Cultural Significance:

Considered sacred in many parts of India, Sheesham wood is used in temples, religious ceremonies, and holy artifacts. It symbolizes strength, endurance, and prosperity.
